In Indian Classical music, a raga is a melodic 'personality'. A raga has an underlying scale and a characteristic melodic framework for joining the notes of that scale which makes it unique, and within that uniqueness, it allows for improvisation just as a person can can do various things, all the time being that same person. There is no directly equivalent term in the Western Classical music. Ragas are associated with various times of the day, various seasons in nature and various moods of the mind. Here is a comprehensive list of ragas of the Hindustani (North Indian) Classical music, self-compiled using resources primarily from https://raagtime.com and http://www.tanarang.com. For some introduction on the notes (S, R, m etc.), see here.
